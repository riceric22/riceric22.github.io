From Sketch to Publication

A Guide to AI-Assisted Scientific Visuals

Getting Started

Your Tools

A dataset, a process description, a whiteboard/paper for sketching, and example visuals from papers.

Your Goal

To experiment with the methods below to create, modify, and caption visuals using Python.

Documentation

Create and keep a Google Doc open in the class vault to record your prompts, code, and analysis. Open Class Vault Folder.

The Forward Pass: From Idea to Python Code

Describe a desired visual and ask the AI to generate the corresponding Python code. For this exercise, please choose one of the experiments below to follow through. You are welcome to explore the others if you have time.

The Reverse Pass: From Image to Code

Use multimodal LLMs to interpret a visual and generate the Python code to replicate it. For this exercise, please choose one of the experiments below to follow through.

The Art of Modification: Iterative Refinement

Use the AI as a coding assistant to modify and build upon existing figures.

The Narrative: Generating Captions and Titles

A visual is only as good as its caption. Test the AI's ability to interpret a visual and describe it accurately.

The Creative Consultant: Brainstorming Visuals

Use the AI for ideation, not just execution, to suggest novel ways to communicate your concepts.

Reflection and Final Analysis

Review your notes, submit your best figure, and consolidate your learnings.

Final Submission

As the final step, please add your best figure from this exercise to our shared class presentation. Create a new slide, add your figure, and include your name.
