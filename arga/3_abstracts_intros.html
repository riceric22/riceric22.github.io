The Art of the Start

An Interactive Guide to AI-Assisted Abstracts & Introductions

Getting Started

Your Tool

A research paper or a near-complete draft that you will use as the source material.

Your Goal

To experiment with the techniques below and generate multiple versions of an abstract and introduction.

Documentation

Keep a Google Doc open to record your prompts, the AI's outputs, and your analysis for each experiment.

The Abstract: The Paper in Miniature

The challenge is to be comprehensive yet concise. Test how different contexts affect the AI's ability to create a strong abstract.

The Introduction: Setting the Stage

The introduction creates a narrative that leads the reader to your research question. Explore how to build this narrative with an AI.

The Synthesis: Crafting a 'One-Shot' Master Prompt

After experimenting, synthesize your learnings into a single, comprehensive set of instructions that could generate a strong first draft in one attempt.

1. Review Your Notes

Look back at all your experiments. Identify the key ingredients that led to success (e.g., specific persona, structural rules, key context to provide).

2. Draft a "Master Prompt"

Combine all successful elements into a single, detailed prompt. Use the template below as a guide.

Master Prompt Template

  • Persona: "Act as an expert academic writer specializing in [Your Field]."
  • Task: "Your task is to generate a [250-word abstract / 5-paragraph introduction] for my research paper."
  • Context: "Use the attached document as your source material. Pay special attention to the [Methods and Results sections / Abstract and Conclusion]."
  • Structural Requirements: "The introduction must follow a funnel structure. The abstract must contain one sentence for each of these components: Background, Methods, Results, Conclusion."
  • Key Information to Include: "Ensure you emphasize that the core finding is [Your Core Finding] and that it addresses the gap of [The Gap in Literature]."
  • Style and Tone: "The tone should be formal, objective, and confident. Avoid using jargon where possible."

3. Test and Analyze

Run your master prompt with the attached paper. How does the output of this single, detailed prompt compare to the drafts you generated through iterative or interactive methods?

Reflection and Final Analysis

Review your notes, get feedback from a peer, and consolidate your learnings from this exercise.

Peer Review & Feedback

Swap your best-generated abstract and introduction with a partner. Read their work and provide constructive feedback based on the questions below. Discuss your findings with each other.

Feedback Questions for Your Partner's Work:

  • Does the abstract clearly and concisely summarize the paper's core components (problem, method, results, conclusion)?
  • Does the introduction present a compelling narrative or 'story' that motivates the research?
  • Is the 'gap' in the existing literature that this research fills made obvious?
  • What is one thing that works particularly well? What is one area for improvement?
